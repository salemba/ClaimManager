ClaimManager UX Design Directions

Six directions for a calm, operational, claimant-safe claims experience

Each direction applies the same product principles: workflow clarity, next-step guidance, early supervisor confidence, and claimant-safe transparency. Compare them by visual weight, information hierarchy, and how clearly they surface blocker, owner, and next action.

Core TestWhich direction makes the next action obvious without overwhelming the user?
Emotional GoalClarity first, then confidence for operators and reassurance for claimants.
ReferencesBorrow calm triage from Asana and readable hierarchy from Notion, not the heaviness of ServiceNow.
PlatformDesktop-first MPA, spacious layouts, modern crisp type, near-real-time operational visibility.

Why this works

Everything unrelated to the immediate workflow question is deferred. The interface keeps users in a tight loop of orient, act, confirm.
Best for

Reducing cognitive load aggressively for users who need a direct, low-distraction operational mode rather than a broad dashboard surface.

How to choose

Pick the direction that best balances first-use clarity, blocker visibility, and calm operational confidence. You can also combine elements, for example: the oversight structure of Direction 1 with the claim-story clarity of Direction 2 and the claimant-safe explanation treatment of Direction 5.
